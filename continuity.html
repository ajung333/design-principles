Gesalt Theory Law No. 3: Continuity

What is Continuity?

Our brain will also continue a pattern beyond its ending points. Our eyes will follow the direction of a line and see a single cross as opposed to four short converging lines. By applying this law, we can add a sense of direction and movement to a layout. (Golombisky, Kim, and Rebecca Hagen. White Space Is Not Your Enemy: a Beginner's Guide to Communicating Visually through Graphic, Web & Multimedia Design. Focal Press/Elsevier, 2010, pp. 76.)

Our brains are wired to follow movement, so if we something like a swooping line, our eyes automatically follow that line. Though the use of lines, we can direct the viewerâ€™s attention from one element to another. Utilizing this law, we can create a sense of direction and movement.

As an artist, lines are something that I pay special attention to. Without lines, I cannot bring attention to certain details or make something stand out. A few swooping lines can project movement of an object and show something as moving rather than stationary. Through these lines, I can show details and movement of the object.
